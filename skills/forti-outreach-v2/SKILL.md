# Skill: forti-outreach-v2 — Modular Fear-Hook Template

**Version:** v2
**Use for:** Defense tech companies, security-vertical software, dual-use technology providers
**Tone:** Direct. Fear-hook framing, but grounded in documented facts. Not alarmist — credible.
**Word limit (email body):** 130 words max
**Israeli angle:** Depends on context. See rules below.

---

## When to select this template

Select v2 when the prospect is:
- A defense technology company (hardware, software, systems integration)
- A security-vertical SaaS or platform vendor
- A dual-use technology provider (e.g., AI applied to surveillance, communications, sensing)
- A company whose customers are governments or defense procurement offices

Do NOT use v2 for hospitality, healthcare, or Gulf sovereign-owned assets. Use v2.2 for those.
Do NOT use v2 in contexts where fear-framing would be culturally inappropriate (UAE C-suite, for example).

---

## Module system

v2 uses a modular hook structure. The agent selects the best-fit module based on research findings.

### Module A — Detection window
For companies whose product or service is in a detection or monitoring category.
Hook: Reference a documented industry-wide detection failure, blind spot, or coverage gap relevant to their category.
Bridge: Eddie advises on closing those windows before they show up in procurement RFPs.

### Module B — Procurement pressure
For companies navigating government or defense procurement.
Hook: Reference a recent tender, procurement shift, regulatory change, or RFP that affects their category.
Bridge: Eddie advises on positioning for procurement outcomes, not just technical specs.

### Module C — Integration risk
For companies whose product integrates into larger defense or security stacks.
Hook: Reference a known integration failure mode or interoperability challenge in their category.
Bridge: Eddie advises on integration architecture decisions that affect long-term contract retention.

### Module D — Leadership-change pivot
For any company that has had a C-suite or VP-level change in the last 12 months.
Hook: New leadership typically re-evaluates advisory relationships. Reference the change directly.
Bridge: Early advisory conversations tend to be most valuable in the first 6 months of a new leadership cycle.

## Structure

### Subject line
Hook-forward. 3-7 words. Specific.
Examples:
- "[Org]'s detection window, Q1 2025"
- "RFP shift: [category], [country]"
- "[New CTO name]'s advisory gap"

### Hook (1-2 sentences)
Fear-adjacent but fully sourced. References a documented industry fact, procurement shift, or product-category reality.
Source URL required. No source = kill the hook, fall back to another module or v2.2.

**Good (Module A):** "In the last two procurement cycles, detection vendors in [category] have lost contracts when their coverage maps didn't hold up under government testing — a pattern Janes documented in their Q4 2024 review."
**Bad:** "Cyberattacks are on the rise and your company could be next."

### Relevance bridge (1 sentence)
One sentence connecting the documented fact to Eddie's advisory role.

### Ask (1 sentence)
Low friction. Same as v2.2 and v2.1.

### Signature
```
Eddie Nudel | Strategic Advisor | Fortline Global
```

## DM Long (standalone)

80-100 words. Module-based hook. Same sourcing rules.

## DM Short — Email Reminder

1-2 sentences. Reference the email. Maintain the direct tone.

## DM Short — Standalone

40-60 words. Hook from any module + ask. Reads like a senior-to-senior LinkedIn message.

---

## Hard rules for v2

- Every fear-hook must cite a source URL. No anonymous industry claims.
- No attack speculation: "could be your company next", "when you get hit".
- No urgency language: "act now", "before it's too late".
- No "100% protection", "guarantee", "fully protected".
- Israeli angle: use judgment based on the prospect's geography and the voice profile. Israeli regulatory context is appropriate if the company operates in Israel or sells to Israeli defense. OFF for non-Israeli companies unless they explicitly reference Israeli partnerships.
- Word limit is 130 words.
- If no module produces a sourced hook, fall back to v2.2. Do not use v2 without a sourced hook.
