# Skill: forti-outreach-v22 — Soft Advisory Template

**Version:** v2.2
**Use for:** UAE/Gulf hospitality, healthcare, aviation, commercial real estate, sovereign-owned assets
**Tone:** Soft advisory. No threat-naming. No fear hooks. Observation + opportunity framing.
**Word limit (email body):** 140 words max
**Israeli angle:** OFF by default for UAE/Gulf verticals. Do not introduce. Do not reference.

---

## When to select this template

Select v2.2 when the prospect's vertical is any of:
- UAE/Gulf luxury or upper-midscale hospitality (hotels, resorts, branded residences)
- UAE/Gulf healthcare (hospitals, clinics, medical cities)
- Gulf aviation (airports, ground handling, MRO)
- Commercial real estate in the Gulf (malls, mixed-use, DIFC/ADGM-registered assets)
- Sovereign or government-adjacent organizations (ADQ, Mubadala portfolio companies, NEOM)

When the vertical is ambiguous, default to v2.2 (more conservative than v2 or v2.1).

---

## Structure

### Subject line
Factual observation framing. 3-7 words. Specific to the org.
Examples:
- "Al Futtaim retail expansion, 2025"
- "Jumeirah Group's new Maldives property"
- "DIFC tower handover, March 2025"

### Hook (1-2 sentences)
A specific, verifiable factual observation about the prospect's organization.
- Reference a recent opening, expansion, acquisition, leadership change, or public mention.
- Must have a source URL. No source = no hook. Kill it.
- Never name a threat or incident. Frame around the opportunity or transition, not the risk.

**Good:** "Saw that [Hotel Group] opened its sixth UAE property in Q4 — congratulations on the Yas Island opening."
**Bad:** "With rising security threats in the Gulf, you need..."
**Bad:** "After the incidents at similar properties..."

### Relevance bridge (1-2 sentences)
Connect the observation to why Eddie's advisory work is relevant right now.
- Frame as strategic timing, not emergency.
- No "you need" language. Prefer "as you scale..." or "the complexity that comes with..."

**Good:** "As you scale across new geographies, the security architecture decisions made in the first 12 months tend to set the baseline for the next decade."
**Bad:** "You need to address this before something goes wrong."

### Ask (1 sentence)
A single, low-friction ask. Conversational.
- "Worth a 20-minute conversation?"
- "Happy to share a few observations if useful."
- "If timing is right, I'd value 15 minutes."

Never: "Book a demo", "Schedule a call via Calendly", "Can we jump on a quick call?"

### Signature
```
Eddie Nudel | Strategic Advisor | Fortline Global
```

## DM Long (standalone)

Same structure, 80-100 words. No subject line — open with the hook. Same tone.

## DM Short — Email Reminder (sent 24h after cold email)

1-2 sentences. Reference that an email was sent. Do not re-pitch.
Example: "Sent an email yesterday about [specific factual thing from hook]. Happy to use LinkedIn if that's easier."

## DM Short — Standalone (LinkedIn-only sequences)

40-60 words. Hook + ask only. No bridge. Reads like a real LinkedIn message.
Example: "Noticed [Org] opened its [location] property — impressive project. I advise hospitality groups on security architecture at the expansion stage. Would a brief exchange be useful?"

---

## Hard rules for v2.2

- Zero threat-naming. Zero incident references. Zero fear language.
- Israeli angle is off. Do not mention Israel, Israeli technology, Israeli partners, or any Israel-adjacent framing.
- No "security gap", "vulnerability", "exposure", "risk" in the subject line.
- Tone should read as a warm peer-to-peer introduction, not a cold sales email.
- If you cannot find a hook that passes these rules, use a leadership change or property milestone as the hook. Never manufacture one.
