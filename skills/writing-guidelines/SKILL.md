# Writing Guidelines — Fortline Global

Applied to all drafts produced by Fortline agents. Non-negotiable.

---

## Voice

Fortline Global is a defense-tech advisory firm. Eddie Nudel is a strategic advisor, not a vendor.
The voice is:
- Precise and brief. Every sentence earns its place.
- Confident, not pushy. Advisory, not sales.
- Peer-to-peer. Assume the reader is a senior decision-maker who has seen every cold pitch.
- Geopolitically literate. Never naive about regional context.
- Understated. The strongest emails never try hard.

---

## Sentence-level rules

- Sentences: short to medium. 15 words max per sentence is a good target.
- No run-ons. Split at the conjunction.
- Active voice. Subject → verb → object.
- No filler openers: "I hope this email finds you well", "I wanted to reach out", "My name is Eddie".
- No corporate jargon: "synergies", "holistic", "leverage", "circle back", "move the needle", "bandwidth".
- No urgency pressure: "act now", "before it's too late", "limited time".
- No security absolutism: "guarantee", "100% protection", "fully protected", "we can stop".
- No speculation about future attacks or incidents.
- No exclamation marks. Ever.
- No emojis. Ever.
- No em-dash (—) or en-dash (–). Use a comma or period instead.
- One ask per email. One CTA. Never two.

---

## Structure

### Email

1. **Hook line (1-2 sentences):** A specific, factual observation about the prospect or their org. Must cite a source. Not a compliment — an observation.
2. **Relevance bridge (1-2 sentences):** Why that observation matters to Eddie's advisory work and to them.
3. **Ask (1 sentence):** A single, low-friction request. "Worth a 20-minute call?" or similar. Never a demo request.
4. **Signature:**
   ```
   Eddie Nudel | Strategic Advisor | Fortline Global
   ```
   No phone. No LinkedIn URL in the email body.

### DM Long (LinkedIn standalone, no prior email)

Same structure as email, compressed. 80-100 words. Subject line becomes the first sentence.

### DM Short — Email Reminder (sent 24h after email)

One or two sentences. References the email without quoting it. Does not introduce new arguments.
Example: "Sent you an email yesterday about [specific thing]. Happy to swap the channel if LinkedIn is easier."

### DM Short — Standalone (LinkedIn-only sequences)

40-60 words. Hook + ask. No bridge. Reads like a real LinkedIn message, not a condensed email.

---

## Subject lines

- 3-7 words. No clickbait. No all-caps.
- Specific to the prospect, not generic ("security strategy" is bad; "Al Futtaim CCTV upgrade, 2024" is better).
- Template version conventions:
  - v2.2: factual observation framing ("Your expansion into DIFC, 2025")
  - v2.1: gap-statement framing ("Security gap at [Org] post-[event]")
  - v2: hook-forward framing ("[Org]'s detection window")

---

## Signature (email only)

```
Eddie Nudel | Strategic Advisor | Fortline Global
```

Nothing else. No tagline. No website. No phone number. No LinkedIn URL.

---

## Things that instantly kill a draft

- Any banned phrase from the runtime list
- Any hook without a source URL
- Any source URL that returns non-200
- Any source URL from the untrusted domains list
- Subject line with an exclamation mark
- Body that addresses the wrong vertical (e.g., v2.1 fear-based framing sent to UAE hospitality)
- More than one ask / CTA
- Body over the word limit for the template version
