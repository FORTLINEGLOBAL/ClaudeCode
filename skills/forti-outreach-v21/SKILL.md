# Skill: forti-outreach-v21 — Gap-Statement Defense Template

**Version:** v2.1
**Use for:** Israel-based defense, Ukraine, energy infrastructure, hospitals where threat-naming is standard practice
**Tone:** Gap-statement. Factual, sober. Identifies a specific documented gap or transition. No hyperbole.
**Word limit (email body):** 120 words max
**Israeli angle:** ON for Israel-based verticals. OFF for all others unless explicitly enabled in voice profile.

---

## When to select this template

Select v2.1 when the prospect operates in:
- Israeli defense or dual-use technology companies
- Ukrainian defense, reconstruction, or energy infrastructure
- Critical energy infrastructure anywhere (refineries, grid operators, pipelines)
- Hospital systems in active conflict zones or post-incident contexts
- Any context where threat-naming is standard industry practice (i.e., the prospect's own communications use this language)

Do NOT use v2.1 for UAE/Gulf contexts. Default to v2.2 for Gulf prospects even if they touch defense supply chains.

---

## Structure

### Subject line
Gap-statement framing. Specific. Sober.
Examples:
- "Detection gap at [Org] post-procurement"
- "Security architecture gap: [specific system or event]"
- "[Org]'s integration window"

Keep it factual. Not alarmist. The reader should recognize it as accurate, not sensational.

### Hook (1-2 sentences)
A documented gap, transition, or known industry pattern that applies to this specific organization.
- Cite a specific event, system upgrade, acquisition, or publicly known capability gap.
- Source URL required. No source = kill the hook.
- Gap-framing only: "As [Org] integrates [system X], the detection coverage during the transition window is typically where advisory value is highest."
- Never speculate about future attacks. Never reference undocumented incidents.

**Good:** "After [Org]'s procurement of [system] last year, the integration phase is where most organizations find their coverage assumptions don't hold."
**Bad:** "You could be the next target of..."
**Bad:** "Before your next incident..."

### Relevance bridge (1 sentence)
One precise sentence. Why Eddie's advisory is relevant to this specific gap.
No preamble. No setup. Just the connection.

**Good:** "I work with teams at this integration stage to pressure-test assumptions before they get locked in."
**Bad:** "Fortline Global specializes in a wide range of security advisory services for..."

### Ask (1 sentence)
Same as v2.2. Low friction. Peer-to-peer.

### Signature
```
Eddie Nudel | Strategic Advisor | Fortline Global
```

## DM Long (standalone)

70-90 words. Gap-statement framing maintained. No threat speculation.

## DM Short — Email Reminder

1-2 sentences. Reference the email. Same sober tone.

## DM Short — Standalone

40-60 words. Gap + ask. Reads like a senior-to-senior LinkedIn message.

---

## Hard rules for v2.1

- Gap-statement = documented gap or transition, not threat speculation.
- No future-attack speculation: "next attack", "when you get hit", "before it's too late".
- No urgency pressure.
- No "you need to" language.
- Israeli angle: ON for Israeli defense verticals (i.e., you may reference Israeli regulatory frameworks, IDF/INCD context, etc., if relevant to the gap). OFF for all other geographies.
- Word limit is 120 words — tighter than v2.2 because the gap-statement delivers precision, not context.
- If no documented gap can be found with a source URL, fall back to v2.2 framing. Do not manufacture a gap.
